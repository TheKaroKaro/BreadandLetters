Last Updated: [Insert Date]

Bread & Letters ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how your personal information is collected, used, and disclosed by us.

1. Information We Collect

We collect information that you directly provide to us, as well as information automatically collected through your use of our services.

A. Information You Provide Directly

  • Contact Information: When you request a consultation, we collect information via the contact form on our site, including your name, email, phone number, number of employees, and industry.
  • Class Registration: When you enroll in an online class, we collect payment information and necessary contact details to manage your access to course materials.
  • Correspondence: We collect personal data contained in any correspondence you send to us.

B. Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage Data: We automatically collect information about your interactions with our website, including the blog, free tools, and class pages (e.g., pages viewed, time spent on pages, links clicked).
  • Cookies and Tracking: We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to track activity on our service and hold certain information.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • **To Provide Services:** To fulfill consulting requests and deliver access to our online classes.
  • **Communication:** To respond to your inquiries, send updates, and market our services to you.
  • **Improvement:** To monitor and analyze usage and trends to improve our website, online classes, and free tools.
  • **Legal Compliance:** To comply with legal obligations and protect our business interests.

3. Sharing and Disclosure

As a sole proprietorship, we do not generally share your information. However, we may share information with:

  • **Service Providers:** Third-party vendors who perform services on our behalf, such as hosting, email delivery (e.g., Formspree), and payment processing (for online classes).
  • **Legal Requirements:** To comply with applicable law, legal process, or governmental requests.

4. Your Choices

You have the right to opt-out of receiving promotional communications from us by following the unsubscribe instructions provided in those communications.
